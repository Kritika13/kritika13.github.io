DigiBlood - UI/UX Case Study

The application is created to encourage current blood donors to continue donating blood while also assisting new blood donors. It also allows users to keep track of all of their health cards and prescriptions at one place.

The Problem

  • The amount of blood donated is a fraction of what is needed.
  • Prescriptions and medical records are not stored online.
  • Blood donation is heavily reliant on family and friends.
  • Blood donation myths, anxieties, and misunderstandings among the general population.

    • Discover

    Quantative Research

  • Around 11 million blood donations are collected in India every year against the required 13.5 million for 1.35 billion population in the country (1 per cent), leaving a gap of nearly 2 million.
  • And due to the lack of customer satisfaction and donor loyalty, repeat donations are also low (0-30 per cent).
  • Every year, India wastes about 6.5 lakh units of blood and blood components.
  • The primary factors that lead to blood being wasted are deterioration during storage and expiry, but can also be due to issues such as broken bags and/or seals, contamination under donations, clotted donations, delays in conducting tests due to staff shortage and improper transport..

    • Pain Points

  • Unable to locate and search for blood bank facility on time
  • Medical history and papers require a lot of physical file storage which is difficult to track.
  • Lack of information about where the camp is being conducted and many other details about the camp are unknown.
  • User wants to know how much blood I have donated till date and how much I can get from blood bank(free/minimal price) when I (user)need it
